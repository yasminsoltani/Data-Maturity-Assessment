# NASA Data Maturity Assessment (DMA)

## Overview

The **NASA Data Maturity Assessment (DMA)** is the agency's first enterprise-wide governance benchmarking tool, designed to evaluate and track data and AI maturity across all NASA centers. The DMA gives the agency a standardized, repeatable way to measure how well organizational units manage, govern, and leverage data as a strategic asset in support of NASA's mission.

This repository contains high-level documentation on the DMA framework, supporting policy research, and a writing sample on AI and data governance in federal agencies.

---

## The Problem

Large federal agencies like NASA operate across dozens of organizational units with vastly different levels of technical capability, data infrastructure, and governance maturity. Translating high-level AI and data policy mandates, such as those outlined in the NIST AI Risk Management Framework and the OECD Recommendation on AI, into operational systems that function consistently across that diversity is a genuinely hard problem.

Without a shared benchmarking tool, agencies have no reliable way to:
- Measure where they stand relative to governance best practices
- Identify gaps that create risk or hinder mission effectiveness
- Track progress over time as policies and technologies evolve
- Hold organizational units accountable to common standards

The DMA was built to address exactly this challenge.

## My Role

I served as **Technical Lead** for the DMA, working within NASA's Chief Data Officer (CDO) and Chief Artificial Intelligence Officer (CAIO) team at NASA Headquarters. My responsibilities spanned both the technical and policy dimensions of the project:

- Co-developed the Data Maturity Framework in collaboration with senior leadership and cross-agency stakeholders, synthesizing input from NASA centers with varying levels of data maturity
- Contributed to the design of the framework's governance pillars, covering areas including data infrastructure, organizational capabilities, AI safety, and responsible AI deployment
- Architected the full technical infrastructure for the assessment platform, including data models, automated workflows, and executive dashboards
- Designed the assessment methodology, question logic, and scoring mechanisms to produce consistent, measurable governance evaluations
- Engaged directly with the Deputy Chief Data Officer and the agency's Data and AI Subject Matter Expert to ensure the framework aligned with federal policy requirements and NASA's strategic priorities

The DMA is currently being deployed across all NASA centers.

## Framework Overview

The NASA Data Maturity Assessment Framework evaluates organizational maturity across several interconnected domains, including data infrastructure, workforce capabilities, AI safety and governance, and responsible data practices. Each domain is assessed against a defined maturity scale, allowing NASA leadership to benchmark current state, identify priorities, and track improvement over time.

The framework draws on established governance standards including:
- **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)** for AI safety, accountability, and risk management principles
- **OECD Recommendation on Artificial Intelligence** for international AI governance alignment
- **IAPP AI Governance in Practice Report** for organizational implementation best practices
- **Federal data governance policies** including the Foundations for Evidence-Based Policymaking Act
